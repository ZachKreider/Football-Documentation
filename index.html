Introduction

Football was a game invinted from the internals of Rugby. Back in the late 1800's at Harvard Univesity a groupd of young men changed and morfied football to the early stage of what it is today.

During the 1920's the American Professional Football Association was born. Today it's known as the National Football League. Fooball is the most watched sport in America with at least 39% of adults picking football as their favorite sport to watch.

Field is played on 120yards by 55 yards. Each team is allowed 53 players more in lower levels. Their are 6 referres that will make sure the game is followed to code and will flag teams who don't follow the rules. Each team is alloted 3 time outs during the game. The game is consisted of 4 - 15 minute quartes and one half time. Games are shorter in the highschool level and lower.
When it began

Harvard University maintained its distance by sticking to a rugby-soccer hybrid called the “Boston Game.” In May 1874, after a match against McGill University of Montreal, the Harvard players decided they preferred McGill’s rugby-style rules to their own. In 1875, Harvard and Yale played their first intercollegiate match, and Yale players and spectators (including Princeton students) embraced the rugby style as well.

Offense

The Offense is made up of 11 players. Typically, an offense is made up of 5 offensive linemen, 1 Quartback, 1 Running back, 3 wide recievers, and 1 tight end. Their primary goal is to score as many points as possible. More on scorint points later.

  • The Offsive Linemen - the work horses - they block/protect the Quarterback/Running back - numbers worn 50-79
  • The Quarterback - Basically the Field General - calls the plays, throws the ball, hands off the ball. Just the overall leader of the Offensive - numbers worn 1-19
  • The Running back - gets the ball from the quarterback either from a hand off or a pass. Helps block in some scenarios depending on the play. Can score a touchdown. - numbers worn 1-49
  • The Wide Recievers - recieves ball from quarterback mainly through the air. Blocks on runnign plays. Can score a Touchdown - numbers worn 1-29 or 80-89
  • The Tight End - Does it all blocker/catcher - Blocks and catches passes from Quarterback - can score touchdowns - numbers worn 40-49 or 80-99
Defense
The Defense is also made up of 11 people. It is usally made up of 4 defensive linemen. 3 linebackers, and 4 defensive backs. Depending on teams you can have more defensive linemen, or linebackers, or defensive backs. Their primary goal of the game is to stop the offensive from scoring points.
  • The Defensive Linemen - Try to throw off the offensive line by attacking the run or rushing after the quarterback. Plays right up on the line of scrimage opposite of the offensive linemen - numbers worn 40-49 or 80-99
  • The Linebacker - plays 4 yards back behind the defenseive linemen. They try to taclke the running back, tight end, or recievers after they recieve the ball. - numbers worn 40-59
  • The Defensive Backs - Play off the recievers, have to stop against passes as well as the run. They usually play in-coverage. numbers worn 1-29
Special Teams

The special teams are made of 11 people just like offensive or defensive. They sometimes will use players who play on either offensive and defensive as well. They always include a kicker of some kind, wether it be a punter or a kicker. I know it may seem confusing but they aren't the same thing.

There is the Kick-off, this is the opening play of the game. where one team will use a kicker to kick the ball off a tee to the opposing team. Both the kick-off team the recieving team have 11 people. The team that kicks off try to prevent the opposing team to get as many yards as possible. While the recieving team try's to gain as many yards possible on the oppening play. This plays also take place after points have been scored.

The Point-After-Touchdown or PAT, this occures when a team scores a touchdown. Whether it be on offense, defense, and even kick-return team or punt-return team.

The Punt and Punt-Return, this occurs when a team is unable to get a new set of downs. This means a team will need to use a punter to kick the ball to the opposing team to get them away for their scoring endzone. The Punt-Return occurs when an offensive is unable to get a new set of downs, and must kick off. The punt-return team will then try to advance the ball of keep it where is coming down at with a fair catch. This is where a player can catch kicked ball without fear of being tackled by the other team.

How to Score

There are 9 possible ways to score points in the game of Football.

  • "Offensive Touchdown - 6 points - This can be made by either the quarterback, running back, or reciever running into the endzone with the ball. This can also be done by the quarterback throwing the ball to the running back, tightend, or a reciever, and them running it in the endzone."
  • Offensive Field Goal - 3 points - This is made when an offense is close enough for the kicker to kick it in. This is usually done when an offense can't score a touchdown and need the points to win.
  • Defensive Touchdown - 6 points - This is achieved with a defensive player either picks a fumbled ball(ball dropped by offensive player during a play) and runs it in for a touchdown. Or an player intercepts the ball from the quarterback who has thrown it.
  • Defensive Safety - 2 points - This is achieved when a player taclkes an offensive player with the ball in their own endzone.
  • Kick-return Touchdown - 6 points - This is achieve when a player on kick off, returns the ball after kicked for a touchdown.
  • Punt-return Touchdown - 6 points - This is achieved when a player on punt-return returns a kicked punt back for a touchdown.
  • Point-after-touchdown - 1 point - This is achieved when a team kicks a ball through the uprights the play after a touchdown that was scored.
  • 2-Point Conversion - This is achieved when a team after a team has scored a touchdown and are trying to get closer to even or win the game. This play is played as a normal offensive play but you only have one shot to get into the endzone for the extra 2 points.
Levels of the Game

The first level of the game is flag football, this entry level to the game where you learn the basics about the game. This game is actually played from 5-50; as the this is the safest form of football as their is no pads or helmets and limited contact.

Second, is like youth football, usually played from 10-13 years of age. This is where you have pads and are tackling other people, really getting physicallity of the game of football.

Third, Highschool football, this is where you play with more advanced players and much more physical game. There are over 1 million highschool football players.

Fourth, is college football, you are at an elite playing level to make it here. Everyone you play with is great and everyone you play against is realy good. There are over 70,000 college football players.

Last, the National Footbal League or the NFL, this is the highest level a football player can make it. This is the best of the very best and everyone is on top. There are only a total of 1,696 players in the NFL. Making it the hardest achievement to be achieved by a football player.
